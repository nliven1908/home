Often, on our visits to the museum, we come back with many unanswered and persistent questions about numerous artifacts, paintings, or objects that we see. We long to integrate them in our thoughts to create and understand our link with the inhabitants of Pangea, beyond our tangible time within the museum confines.  

2

Here is where we step in

With Augmented Reality, we aim to affect and alter the same. Our end goal is to create a museum space with seamless virtual interaction and learning opportunities for the user.  

3

Solution for maximum usability interaction

For the initial testing, the prototype will focus on a historical/cultural setting in order to see how people interact with the same with an educational perspective in mind.

Post initial testing and research revamp , we hope to extend and use the prototype design in multiple forms for educational and recreational purposes to cater to evolving socio-cultural museum-fiction. Incorporating AR glasses as the sole mediator, the user will be able to traverse seamlessly in and out revamp, various times and universes created just for them.  

4

Simple and seamless:

Objects will be brought to life using Blender and set into their respective environments using Unity. Here, the user may choose to walk in a museum space created for them or to interact with paintings and artifacts to understand them better.

We believe this lifelike interaction will result in better affective applications of the thinking process in the educational framework as compared to only the favored modes of reading and listening (as the cornerstones of learning) and will generate interest in the past, such that history will become the buzzword post the millennial era.
